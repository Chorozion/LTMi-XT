# Cold Storage Operations Manual — internal draft

The Cold Storage line is the high-margin part of the business. We currently
operate three trucks across the metro area. Each truck is staffed with one
driver and one technician on long routes. Last quarter we lost two
shipments to coolant mismatch which cost us roughly $4,800 in claims plus
loss of the customer relationship for one of the two.

The standard workflow is: customer calls in, dispatch logs the pickup,
driver collects, technician verifies temperature on arrival, we deliver,
the customer signs for it. It works most of the time. The failure mode
is usually when the technician forgets to verify the gel pack count
before the truck leaves the dock. We added a checklist last month but
compliance has been spotty.

For dry ice loads we always use the VIP panel inserts. They cost more up
front but the failure rate is essentially zero. Gel pack loads are
cheaper but only safe for transit times under 18 hours. Anything longer
should be dry ice.

The newer Sophia Key 3M tool can model a lane and tell us in advance
which configuration will hold. We are not using it on every load yet. The
tool is fast — it returns a recommendation in under a second — but the
team needs training on it.

For routing, we currently let drivers pick their own route. Telematics
shows that route choice varies by 12 to 30 percent in delivered miles
between drivers on the same lane. We think a routing layer would
recover most of that gap.
